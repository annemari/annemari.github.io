Education

After finishing high school, I decided to apply for Viljandi Culture Academy to study acting. Spoiler alert – I did not get in. Having done theatre for most of my high school years, I had no idea what to do next. Thankfully(?), my mom had a dream about me studying law.

The first semester really showed me how dry it was and that did not work for me at all. I was already interested in the front-end part of web development back then. Since high school, I had been involved with many creative writing and roleplay forums and loved designing them using HTML and CSS.

After dropping out from law school, I moved to Tartu. A friend of mine told me about sociology and I realised that this was meant for me, so I started shool again. Life had its own plans, though, and I had to quit my studies in the spring of 2015.

I heard about the opportunity to study web development part-time at the Tartu Vocational Education Centre and as I was working at the time, that sounded like the perfect oppurtunity to work and study at the same time. Sadly, I realised pretty quickly that I couldn't give a 100 per cent both at work and at school, so I decided to quit my job and study full time. And then COVID hit, we went into lockdown twice in two years, and I wasn't able to keep up with my studies, so I had to drop out.

During my studies, I learned a lot about web development and got to meet some amazing people and see great companies.

Work experience

I had to test the functionality of used Android and iOS phones, sometimes also iPads and smartwatches. I had to be fast and systematic. I learned a lot about phones and even got to fix some of them.

I had to make salads, appetisers, and deserts. It was important to make sure my work environment was clean at all times. I learned how to work in a team while completing my individual tasks so that the whole order would come together.

I've also worked as a phone telemarketer and a baker. I’ve been a customer service representative in a café, at a fast food restaurant, a festival catering service, and a meat counter at a supermarket. My work experience might not showcase my skills in IT, but I’ve learned many things over the years. Among other things, I’ve learned to work alone as well as in a team. My work experience has thought me how to manage conflicts and handle different people. I’m glad I have so many different experiences – it enriches my knowledge.
